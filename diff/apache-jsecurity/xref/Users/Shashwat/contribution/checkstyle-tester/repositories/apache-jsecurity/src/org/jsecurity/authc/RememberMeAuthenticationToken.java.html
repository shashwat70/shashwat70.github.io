19  package org.jsecurity.authc;
20  
21  /**
22   * An <tt>AuthenticationToken</tt> that indicates if the user wishes their identity to be remembered across sessions.
23   *
24   * <p>Please note however that when a new session is created for the corresponding user, that user's identity would be
25   * remembered, but they are <em>NOT</em> considered authenticated:
26   *
27   * <p>Authentication is the process of proving you are who you say you are.  In a RememberMe scenario, a remembered
28   * identity gives the system an idea who that person probably is, but in reality, has no way of guaranteeing the
29   * remembered identity <em>really</em> is that user.
30   *
31   * <p>So, although many parts of the application can still perform user-specific logic based on the remembered
32   * identity, such as customized views, it should never perform security-sensitive operations until the user has
33   * actually executed a successful authentication attempt.
34   *
35   * <p>We see this paradigm all over the web, and we'll use <tt>amazon.com</tt> as an example:
36   *
37   * <p>When you visit Amazon.com and perform a login and ask it to 'remember me', it will set a cookie with your
38   * identity.  If you don't log out and your session expires, and you come back, say the next day, Amazon still knows
39   * who you <em>probably</em> are: you still see all of your book and movie recommendations and similar user-specific
40   * features since these are based on your (remembered) user id.</p>
41   *
42   * <p>BUT, if you try to do some sensitive operations, such as access your account's billing data, Amazon forces you
43   * to do an actual log-in, requiring your username and password.
44   *
45   * <p>This is because although amazon.com assumed your identity from 'remember me', it recognized that you were not
46   * actually authenticated.  The only way to really guarantee you are who you say you are, and therefore able to
47   * access sensitive account data, is for you to perform an actual authentication.
48   *
49   * @author Les Hazlewood
50   * @since 0.9
51   */
52  public interface RememberMeAuthenticationToken extends AuthenticationToken {
53  
54      /**
55       * Returns <tt>true</tt> if the submitting user wishes their identity (principal(s)) to be remembered
56       * across sessions, <tt>false</tt> otherwise.
57       *
58       * <p>Please see the class-level JavaDoc for what 'remember me' vs. 'authenticated' means - they are semantically
59       * different.
60       *
61       * @return <tt>true</tt> if the submitting user wishes their identity (principal(s)) to be remembered
62       *         across sessions, <tt>false</tt> otherwise.
63       */
64      boolean isRememberMe();
65  
66  }
